Middle Eastern Capital, Southeast Asian Computing: An Ongoing Industrial Relocation

As Saudi money looks beyond Singapore for an exit, Thailand quietly becomes the answer — Southeast Asia's IDC opportunity under three overlapping windows

📅 2026-06-02 ⏱ ~10 min read 🏷️ Middle Eastern Capital · Southeast Asia IDC · Token Factory · Geopolitics
Middle Eastern Capital Southeast Asia IDC Token Factory Geopolitics AI Compute
⚠️ Core Conclusion: Southeast Asia's IDC transformation into AI computing platforms is essentially an opportunity created by three overlapping windows — Middle Eastern capital's hedging demand + AI inference computing boom + Southeast Asia's electricity cost advantage — this window is expected to last 3-5 years.

1Why Middle Eastern Capital is Moving

In early 2026, the geopolitical situation in the Middle East kept many awake at night. Tensions between Iran and multiple countries continued to escalate, with a noticeable decline in regional security. This is not the first large-scale outflow of Middle Eastern capital—historically, every regional conflict has been accompanied by a round of capital flight for safety.

But this time there's a new variable: Southeast Asia is no longer just playing a "recipient" role; it is becoming an actively attractive destination.

🌏 Five Reasons Southeast Asia is Becoming the Destination

  • Relatively stable politics: Thailand, Vietnam, Indonesia are not drawn into major geopolitical conflicts
  • Robust economic growth: Southeast Asia GDP growth maintained at 4%-6% from 2024-2026
  • Friendly digital economy policies: Thailand's BOI tax exemption policy, Malaysia's Multimedia Super Corridor, Singapore's Smart Nation initiative
  • Labor cost advantage: Compared to the Middle East (excluding energy subsidies) and Singapore, Southeast Asia's overall operational costs are significantly lower
  • Chinese business networks: Chinese business communities in Bangkok, Jakarta, and Ho Chi Minh provide a natural cultural bridge for Middle Eastern Arab capital

🎬 From Cabinet Rental to Token Factory

  • Traditional IDC: Charged per kWh, "cabinet rental" model
  • Token Factory: Jensen Huang's 2025 concept, essentially a factory producing AI inference results
  • Metric shift: From "cabinet rental rate" → "Token output per watt" and "revenue per token"

⚡ Thailand vs Middle East: Why Actual Electricity Costs are Lower

  • PUE gap: Middle Eastern desert climate peaks at 50°C in summer, typical air-cooled PUE 1.8-2.2; Thailand's tropical climate can control PUE at 1.5, liquid cooling can drop to 1.2-1.3
  • Policy stability: Middle Eastern energy subsidies can be adjusted anytime; Saudi Arabia cut once in 2023; Thailand's BOI tax exemptions are 8 years, black and white
  • Comprehensive package advantages: Moderate but stable electricity prices + suitable climate for cooling + strong BOI incentives + geographical position radiating across entire Southeast Asia

5Who is Entering the Market

🏢 Entry Strategies of Three Types of Players

  • Computing platforms originating from Chinese IDCs (started transformation in 2019): Pre-designed power density (30kW+/cabinet), supporting GPU cluster deployment; AI-related revenue share rose from 15%→35% in 2024
  • Southeast Asian hyperscale IDC operators (backed by top PE + sovereign funds): Large-scale land acquisition + standardized design + rapid construction, compressing single campus construction cycle to 12-18 months, customer retention rate >95%
  • Indonesian data center operator (new moves in 2025): Formed a joint venture with the largest local telecom operator, planning a 500MW AI data center campus, driven by renewable energy + high power density + advanced liquid cooling

🎯 Key Takeaways

  • Three overlapping windows: Middle Eastern capital hedging + AI inference computing boom + Southeast Asia's electricity cost advantage, this window is expected to last 3-5 years
  • Thailand is the core destination: Most solid comprehensive package advantages — moderate but stable electricity prices, climate suitable for cooling, strong BOI incentives, geographical position radiating across Southeast Asia
  • Token Factory business logic holds: H100 at 70% utilization achieves annualized net profit of about $860,000 per card; key is scale and management
  • Five conditions for successful transformation: Power density design (30kW+/cabinet) + GPU resource lock-in + pre-established customer relationships + compliance system + liquid cooling retrofit
  • Execution sits between opportunity and success: Everyone sees the opportunity; local IDC's differentiation advantage must be built on dimensions of cost, localized relationships, and policy utilization
