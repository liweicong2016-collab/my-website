2UAE: G42-led AI strategy

G42 (Group 42) is the linchpin of the UAE's AI strategy. Backed by Abu Dhabi sovereign funds (Mubadala, etc.), it has grown into MENA's largest AI company.

🔍 G42's strategic characteristics

  • Sovereign AI approach: G42 prioritizes data sovereignty; all AI infrastructure must run on UAE sovereign clouds
  • Global partnerships: Deep collaboration with Microsoft (which invested $1.5B in G42), while also procuring chips from Chinese vendors (HW, etc.)
  • Healthcare AI as vanguard: G42 Healthcare has become the UAE's largest healthcare AI company, with world-leading testing capacity demonstrated during the pandemic
  • US export control pressure: In 2024, the US intensified chip export scrutiny on G42, demanding it sever ties with Chinese vendors

  • NEOM project: One of the world's largest AI-driven city projects, with plans for extensive AI infrastructure deployment

5Chip export controls and MENA AI competition

US chip export controls are the single biggest variable in MENA's AI competitive landscape. Since 2022, A100/H100 have been restricted; in 2024, H20 was added to the control list. This has had profound implications for the MENA region.

�hambra🛰️ Impact of chip controls on MENA

  • Countries within the US alliance system (UAE, Israel): The US permits exports but imposes restrictive conditions (e.g., requiring G42 to reduce cooperation with China)
  • Neutral countries (Saudi Arabia, Qatar): The US tacitly allows chip procurement from other nations but demands no onward transfer to China
  • Opportunity for Chinese vendors: HW Ascend series has found a new market in MENA; Chinese GPU startups are also using MENA as their first overseas beachhead
  • Regional compute platforms: Chip controls have created a regional compute supply gap in MENA, enabling IDCs with GPU resources to command premium pricing

🎯 Key Takeaways

  • G42 (UAE) is the center of gravity for MENA AI, but faces US export control pressure and is seeking to diversify chip procurement
  • Saudi PIF is the world's most active sovereign AI investor, pursuing a dual-track approach via the NEOM project and outbound investments
  • US chip controls have created a regional compute supply gap, allowing IDCs with GPU resources to earn steady premiums
  • Chinese AI firms are using MENA as their first overseas beachhead, with HW Ascend series finding a new market in the region
  • MENA AI competition is an extension of US-China AI rivalry; understanding great-power dynamics is key to grasping MENA opportunities
