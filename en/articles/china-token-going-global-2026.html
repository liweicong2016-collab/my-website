From 1.2% to 61%—The Real Questions Behind the Share: Who Is Going Global, How to Monetize, and Can It Last?

EN Summary：Chinese open-source LLMs grew from 1.2% to 61% OpenRouter token share in 18 months. This report dissects who is actually going global, which markets matter, the pricing war at 1/30 of OpenAI, the 4 proven monetization paths, and 3 counter-intuitive findings (the real share is probably ~45% globally when AWS/Azure/Vertex are included; the biggest winners are middle-layer players like Together AI, not the model companies; open-source is the biggest lever AND the biggest risk).
⚠️ Core Conclusion: Chinese models capturing 61% share on OpenRouter is just the surface. The real global share is likely close to 45% (after including AWS Bedrock / Azure / Vertex). The biggest winners aren't the fastest model companies, but middle-layer players like Together AI and OpenRouter that "act as overseas intermediaries." The twin engines of open-source + extreme low pricing are nearing their ceiling; the next step is a full-stack war of SaaS + hardware bundling.

📌 What truly determines the share is the third one

Developers won't register for a Chinese cloud account—they directly switch APIs on OpenRouter with one click. Going global isn't a sales problem; it's an infrastructure problem: whoever puts together the quartet of weights + inference compute + OpenAI-compatible interface + Stripe billing first captures passive traffic.

🔀 Path B — Aggregation Platform Revenue Share (Startups)

🖥️ Path D — Hardware + Model Bundling (IDC / Chip Manufacturers)

Revenue $100 million–$3 billion/year; gross margin 20–35%; chip + server + optical module manufacturers bundle Chinese models to sell into Southeast Asia / Middle East / Latin America

6Three Counter-intuitive Findings

💡 Finding 1 — The Real Global Share May Already Be Close to 45%

OpenRouter's public 61% data excludes the part being "pushed through AWS Bedrock / Azure AI Foundry / Vertex Model Garden / Vercel/Cloudflare Gateways." Adding these implicit calls, the actual share of Chinese models in global LLM inference traffic may already be close to 45%.

💡 Finding 2 — The Most Successful Globalizers Are Middle Layer Players

Together AI annualizes $500 million ARR with 60%+ gross margin, essentially selling Chinese open-source models back to the US. The early OpenRouter team is half Chinese heritage. Cloudflare Workers AI uses Chinese models as "low-latency alternatives" sold to European and American SaaS. Conclusion: A significant portion of the globalization money is earned by "Chinese diaspora teams acting as intermediaries overseas"—the AI version of the "overseas Chinese warehouse" model in cross-border e-commerce.

💡 Finding 3 — Open-Source Is the Biggest Lever and Biggest Risk

Three landmines: (1) Closed-source A / B series systematically distill Chinese open-source model outputs; (2) The US Congress began discussing adding "model weights" to the BIS control list in 2025; (3) Once developers get used to the OpenAI-compatible protocol + Stripe + AWS deployment, their coupling with Chinese models is actually very low—they can switch back overnight. The true winners are those with a full-stack layout of "open-source + closed-source + SaaS + hardware."

7Judgments on AI Going Global Frontlines

Judgment 1 — Customer purchasing behavior splits into two layers
• Individual developers / Early startups: Pay by token → Chinese models = default option
• Mid-tier SaaS / Enterprise clients: Pay by "business outcome" → Chinese models = backup + negotiation chip
Sales strategy needs two sets: one for developers (GitHub / Discord / HN), one for procurement decision-makers (compliance + data residency + local case studies)
Judgment 2 — Three underestimated market opportunities
1. Southeast Asian multilingual customer service (Indonesian / Vietnamese / Thai)
2. Spanish-language coding education in Latin America (Mexico / Brazil K12 + vocational training)
3. Middle East Arabic language + Islamic culture scenarios (financial compliance / Sharia advisory / content moderation)
Judgment 3 — Reverse globalization is where the real money is
Domestic MaaS players have domestic revenue of 10–15 billion RMB/year, while overseas API revenue is less than 1/10. Overseas API is a path of price wars + thin margins. The real money is in pulling overseas developers into the domestic ecosystem (WAIC / Yunqi Conference / Hangzhou / Suzhou / Shenzhen AI startup parks).
Judgment 4 — Legal war signals to be wary of
In Q4 2025, a leading American AI company began taking legal action over being "diverted by Chinese open-source models" (training data compliance tracing). All major overseas client contracts should reserve a "model switching clause" and a "geopolitical force majeure clause".

🎯 Key Takeaways

  • Token Going Global ≠ Model Going Global ≠ API Going Global: What truly determines share is indirect calls on third-party platforms (OpenRouter / Vercel / Cloudflare), not direct sales by Chinese cloud vendors
  • 61% is surface, real share likely close to 45%: OpenRouter doesn't include Chinese model calls on AWS Bedrock / Azure / Vertex
  • The biggest winners are middle-layer players: Together AI ($500 million ARR, 60% gross margin) "selling Chinese open-source models back to the US" is 100 times easier than going global directly
  • Pricing war at 1/30 is now the norm: Cache hit at 0.014 USD per million tokens—an order of magnitude lower than OpenAI's batch API
  • Four monetization paths: Direct API sales / Aggregation revenue share / Industry SaaS / Hardware bundling—only a "full-stack layout" is the endgame
  • Three underestimated opportunities: Southeast Asian multilingual customer service / Latin American coding education / Middle Eastern Arabic scenarios
  • Open-source is the biggest lever and biggest risk: Being distilled + BIS controls + low coupling—three landmines to dismantle
